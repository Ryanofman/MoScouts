About Me

From Player to Professional Scout

My journey in football began long before Yale. Growing up in Spring, Texas, I developed a deep understanding of the game through years of competition at Klein Oak High School, where I earned First-Team All-District honors and helped lead our team to two Bi-District Championships.

At Yale, I didn't just play football – I studied it. As a linebacker who earned All-Ivy Honorable Mention and received the Norman S. Hall Memorial Trophy, I learned to read the game from the inside out. This experience, combined with my Economics degree, gives me a unique edge in modern scouting.

Now, I apply that same analytical mindset and competitive drive to identifying talent. My playing experience allows me to see beyond the statistics – understanding the mental aspects, leadership qualities, and intangibles that separate good players from great ones.
